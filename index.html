    About Me

    I graduated from NC State University in May of 2017 with degrees in Biomedical and Electrical Engineering. My diverse engineering coursework and previous experiences coupled with my background in biomedical engineering allow me to bring in a unique perspective, attempting to bride the gap between man and machine. I have experience developing, implementing, and testing code in both assembly languages and C/C++/Python as well as extensive coding in MATLAB for data mining and analyzing. I also successfully led three design teams in designing and manufacturing a wearable continuous-vitals monitoring device, an upper limb closed loop force feedback system, and an efficient micro heat-sink for a self-powered wearable biosensor. Working alongside these multidisciplinary teams of material engineers, computer engineers, chemists, and mechanical engineers has exposed me to the multiple variables and stakeholders that comprise project teams.
