    Projects

    Continual Care

    In it I proposed a continuous monitoring device, specifically to aid first responders and nurses with administering vitals tests without much disruption to the patient. Dubbed "Continual Care", this wearable battery-powered device, driven by an array of sensors, was designed with the intention of introducing import it into the Biomedical market. My job was to lead the design and manufacturing team. The circuit consisted of an Arduino, Infrared emitter/detectors, LED screen, and a moveable tilted metal bar with two pressure sensors. Measuring the blood oxygen content was don with an IR LED and dectector. Measuring the heart rate was done as a byproduct of measuring the blood pressure. Coninuous noninvasive blood pressue was a large puzzle to solve, in the end, I came up with a tilted bar with two pressure sensors. When the device was strapped to the wrist the inclined pressure sensor would be taken as a reference point while the declined section read the pressure exerted by the blood vessels. This, in turn, created a blood pressure pulse wave in which the device analyzed 5-second blocks of time and output the Systolic and Diastolic values. As a final note, this project was completed in conjunction with North Carolina State Univeristy's department of Entrepreneurship.
